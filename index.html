Quantum Computing: The Next Frontier

This webpage is hosted online using GitHub Pages to explore how subatomic particles will change the world.

What is the technology?

Quantum computing uses the principles of quantum mechanics to process information. While a normal computer uses "bits" (0 or 1), a quantum computer uses qubits. Thanks to a property called superposition, a qubit can exist as both a 0 and a 1 at the same time.

Real-world applications

Quantum computers aren't for playing video games; they are for solving "impossible" problems. Here is where they are used today and in the near future:

  • Medicine: Simulating molecules to create new vaccines in days rather than years.
  • Cybersecurity: Creating unhackable codes (and potentially breaking current ones).
  • Finance: Running complex risk assessments for global markets.

Quantum vs. Classical Computing

Feature Classical Computer Quantum Computer
Basic Unit Bits (0 or 1) Qubits (Superposition)
Speed Linear Exponential for specific tasks

Social impacts

The impact of quantum computing is a double-edged sword. Locally, it could lead to high-paying jobs in research. Globally, it could solve environmental issues by discovering materials that capture carbon from the atmosphere.

However, there are ethical concerns regarding privacy. If a quantum computer can break all current encryption, our personal data and bank accounts could be at risk if the technology falls into the wrong hands.
