Quantum Computing: Beyond the Bit

This webpage is hosted via GitHub Pages. It explores how quantum mechanics is revolutionizing digital technology.

What is the technology?

Quantum computing uses qubits instead of traditional bits. While a normal computer bit is either a 0 or a 1, a qubit uses a property called superposition to be both at the same time. This allows the computer to calculate millions of possibilities simultaneously.

Real-world applications

  • Drug Discovery: Simulating chemical reactions at an atomic level to find cures.
  • Logistics: Finding the most efficient routes for global shipping fleets instantly.
  • Climate Science: Designing new materials that can capture carbon from the air.

Social & Ethical Impacts

The impacts of quantum computing are widespread:

Positive: It can solve global energy crises by discovering more efficient battery materials. Negative/Ethical: It poses a threat to Cybersecurity. Because quantum computers are so fast, they could potentially crack the encryption that protects our bank accounts and private messages.

For more information, visit the Nature Journal of Quantum Research.
